The Short Version

A rare overlap

Most creative technologists can't say they've delivered under the highest-stakes, most governed conditions imaginable. Most senior creative leaders in finance can't ship a real-time 3D scene or a code-built video pipeline. I sit in the overlap — a creative leader with fifteen-plus years directing global teams, who never stopped being a maker.

My work runs from executive multimedia and capital-markets storytelling for regulated institutions, to hands-on motion, generative art and AI production. The thread is the same at every scale: marry a strategic narrative to production excellence, and hold both to the same standard of taste.

Why This Exists

Prompt Atelier, in my own words

Prompt Atelier was created by Aline Cunico to show what one person can make when AI is treated as a craft, not a shortcut. Every episode is a build in the open — an AI-hosted video, a Pixar-style podcast produced from a spreadsheet, a paper-collage explainer, a real-time 3D landing page, a dozen hand-coded generative studies.

It's a working answer to a question I care about professionally: how do you keep taste, rigour and originality when the tools make everything fast and everyone has the same ones? The atelier is where I pressure-test that — no team, no budget, just the tools, the prompts, and the finishing. The name says the belief: an atelier isn't where things are produced, it's where they're finished.
